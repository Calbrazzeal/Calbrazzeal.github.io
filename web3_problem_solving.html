Web3 is the next generation of the internet, powered by blockchain and decentralized technologies. It offers new opportunities for developers, entrepreneurs, and innovators who want to create and use applications that are more secure, transparent, and fair. Web3 is trying to solve some of the fundamental problems that Web2 presents, such as: Content rights: Web2 platforms often take away the ownership and control of content creators, who have to rely on intermediaries or centralized services to monetize their work. Web3 platforms aim to restore the rights of content creators, who can directly own and distribute their content using smart contracts and tokens12. Security: Web2 platforms are vulnerable to hacking, censorship, manipulation, and data breaches. Web3 platforms use cryptography and consensus mechanisms to ensure the integrity and immutability of data and transactions. Web3 platforms also enable users to have more privacy and anonymity by using encryption and pseudonyms12.
